Walked in as the intern. Became the first product manager the company ever had — the day‑0 product person at a medical‑supply commerce startup, owning systems end‑to‑end for 20 months.

open the file

The setting: a lean startup selling medical supplies to clinics and hospitals — sales running on phone calls, field engineers on paper, no product function at all. I got to build one.

  • The CRM (my intern project that became my job): implemented LeadSquared across the full lead‑to‑order cycle with 50+ no‑code automations. First‑response time fell 80% — to 4 hours — and conversions rose 50%. I owned it end‑to‑end from that day on.
  • Auto‑Dialer & Scheduler: reps were hand‑dialing and cherry‑picking leads. Built a dialer + scheduler running on live CRM data — connected calls up 40%, manual work down 50%.
  • The SE App: field service engineers ran on 25+ separate Google Forms. Replaced them with one app — engineer productivity up 32%.
  • Client ticketing: grievances lived in phone calls and memory. Built the ticketing system end‑to‑end — resolution efficiency up 8%, NPS with it.
  • Lead Warmth Intelligence: designed a 15+‑parameter scoring logic that ranked every lead’s readiness to buy — sales stopped guessing whom to call first.
  • The growth track: owned a ₹35–50L marketing budget, replaced the external agency with in‑house ops, and took ROAS from 5 to 25 — CPIL down 18%, ~20,000 leads, a ₹2.1 Cr pipeline, and a 100% jump in lead‑to‑interested ratio across 10+ A/B campaigns.

The ending: Zoplar wound down in early 2025 — startups do that. Every system above was mine end‑to‑end, and the reps came with me.
